# clawin-research-analysis

Define the analytical question first: what decision is being supported, what entities or markets are in scope, what time horizon matters, and what counts as credible evidence.

## Run the analysis

1. Frame the question, hypotheses, and decision criteria.
2. Build a source plan: primary vs. secondary sources, required data points, refresh needs.
3. Collect evidence with timestamps, inclusion rules, and source notes.
4. Validate consistency across sources; flag stale, weak, or conflicting inputs.
5. Analyze using the right method: benchmark, screen, compare, trend, monitor, or scenario-test.
6. Synthesize findings with assumptions, confidence level, and open questions.

## Use practical structures

- Research brief or market scan
- Comparison table with consistent criteria
- Watchlist / monitoring digest
- Scenario grid with assumptions and implications
- Evidence log with source freshness
- Executive summary with confidence markers

## Output pattern

Use:
- **Question / decision supported**
- **Scope and method**
- **Sources and evidence quality**
- **Key findings**
- **Comparisons / scenarios / trends**
- **Implications**
- **Confidence, assumptions, open questions**

## Quality checks

- Keep fact, estimate, and interpretation separate.
- Use the same rubric across all entities being compared.
- State uncertainty instead of hiding it.
- Escalate when evidence is too weak, contradictory, or outdated to support a decision confidently.
- Make it easy for a reviewer to trace conclusions back to sources and assumptions.
