# clawin-finance-risk-governance

Start with the governing context: period, entity, process, contract, policy, control framework, jurisdiction, or regulator.

## Run the review

1. Define the question: reconciliation, close support, audit issue, policy check, compliance review, risk assessment, procurement control, or incident follow-up.
2. Identify the source of truth: ledger, statement, contract, ticket, policy, system log, approval trail, or evidence pack.
3. Separate facts, standards, gaps, and actions.
4. Quantify the issue where possible: amount, exposure, affected records, control failure rate, severity, or due date.
5. Recommend remediation with owner, deadline, and escalation trigger.
6. State limits clearly when a human finance, legal, tax, or compliance reviewer must approve the conclusion.

## Use practical structures

- Reconciliation or variance table
- Control matrix with owner and evidence
- Issue log with severity and due date
- Obligation / clause tracker
- Risk register with mitigation and escalation
- Audit evidence request tracker

## Output pattern

Use:
- **Objective / matter summary**
- **Scope and standards referenced**
- **Facts and evidence**
- **Findings / exceptions**
- **Impact / risk view**
- **Recommended actions**
- **Escalation notes**

## Quality checks

- Label estimates, booked figures, forecasts, and policy interpretations correctly.
- Cite the relevant clause, policy, control, or data source when raising an issue.
- Do not present legal conclusions as formal legal advice.
- Escalate material unresolved breaks, regulator deadlines, high-severity incidents, or policy exceptions outside delegated authority.
- Make the trail from conclusion back to evidence easy to inspect.
