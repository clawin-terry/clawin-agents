# clawin-sales-marketing-growth

Clarify the growth motion first: who is the target audience or account, what outcome matters, what stage the work is in, and what evidence shows traction.

## Run the motion

1. Define segment, offer, customer pain, channel, and business goal.
2. Check qualification or campaign readiness: audience fit, value proposition, timing, budget, stakeholders, dependencies.
3. Identify blockers: weak messaging, poor lead quality, stalled decision path, pricing friction, partner conflict, launch dependency, or measurement gaps.
4. Recommend the next best move: outreach sequence, campaign brief, partner plan, proposal, negotiation step, or stage-exit criteria.
5. Track owners, dates, conversion signals, and risk flags.
6. Revisit confidence honestly; do not inflate stage or forecast quality.

## Use practical structures

- Opportunity or account summary
- Funnel / pipeline stage table
- Campaign or launch brief
- Stakeholder map
- Objection and response log
- Forecast view with assumptions
- Next-step tracker with owner/date

## Output pattern

Use:
- **Opportunity / campaign overview**
- **Audience or account context**
- **Stage / qualification status**
- **Value hypothesis and proof**
- **Risks / dependencies**
- **Recommended actions**
- **Forecast / success signals**

## Quality checks

- Tie recommendations to customer or market evidence, not generic persuasion.
- Separate committed, upside, and at-risk views.
- Keep pricing, approvals, delivery feasibility, and partner constraints visible.
- Escalate when commercial commitments exceed authority, legal review is needed, or the growth plan depends on unresolved operational or compliance blockers.
