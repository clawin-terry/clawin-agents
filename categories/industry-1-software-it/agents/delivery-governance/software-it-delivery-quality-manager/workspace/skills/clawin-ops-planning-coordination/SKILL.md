# clawin-ops-planning-coordination

Frame the operating objective first: what must move, who owns it, what service level or milestone matters, and what is blocked.

## Run the work

1. Define scope, customers, owners, dependencies, and success metrics.
2. Map the workflow: inputs, steps, handoffs, approvals, outputs.
3. Check current state: backlog, throughput, SLA, defects, aging, missed milestones.
4. Identify the main constraint: capacity, unclear ownership, vendor issue, bad handoff, weak process control, or missing decision.
5. Recommend the smallest workable operating mechanism: queue rules, cadence, escalation path, checklist, tracker, or decision forum.
6. Close with owners, dates, measures, and follow-up rhythm.

## Use practical structures

- Process map or swimlane for handoffs
- RAID log for projects and cross-functional delivery
- SLA/KPI table for service operations
- Exception log for recurring failures
- Capacity or staffing table for peak-load planning
- Milestone tracker for PMO and implementation work

## Output pattern

Use:
- **Objective**
- **Current-state summary**
- **Workflow / service scope**
- **Bottlenecks and risks**
- **Recommended actions**
- **Owners, dates, metrics**
- **Escalations / dependencies**

## Quality checks

- Make ownership explicit.
- Distinguish one-off incidents from systemic issues.
- Tie recommendations to measurable service or delivery outcomes.
- Keep governance light unless the decision risk justifies more structure.
- Escalate when approvals, vendor commitments, contractual service levels, or unresolved cross-team blockers exceed the role's authority.
